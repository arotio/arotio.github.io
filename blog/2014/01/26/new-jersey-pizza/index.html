New Jersey Pizza

Story

“I’ll take a large plain pie.” In Jersey, this statement will get you set up with a piping hot disk of deliciousness like the one above. Since moving to Saint Louis in 2013, all this request has gotten us has been puzzled looks. Best case scenario and you have someone who understands, you get a nice flat square-cut Saint Louis style pizza covered in Provel ® (a mixture of cheddar, Swiss, and provolone) like this “beauty” below: Not being satisfied with the above, we decided to seek out a place that would make it like home. We found two spots, Racanelli’s New York Style Pizza and Ferraro’s Jersey Style Pizza (they even have Taylor Ham/Pork Roll!). Both places are good, but this led me to another question – if I love to cook so much, then why haven’t I mastered one of my favorite things to eat? So I set out with the initial goal to make something that tastes as good as the average at home. I think I’ve finally accomplished that, what better way to reintroduce rotio/food to the world.

Recipe

New Jersey Pizza

rotio
Published: January 26, 2014
This is a classic Jersey style pie. Cost for a medium comes out to about $2.50
5.0 2 reviews
Prep time (including overnight rise): 24 hours
Cook time: 6 min
Total time: 24 hour 6 min
Yield: 2 (medium) 14” pies Serving size: 1 medium pie Calories per serving: 600 Fat per serving: 4g

Equipment:

Pizza Stone

Ingredients:

Dough:
Bread Flour: 390g (3c)
Water: 8.5oz
Yeast: 1.75g
Salt: 8g (1.5tsp)
Sugar: 4g (1tsp)
Olive Oil: 1.5tsp

Sauce: (this makes a lot, freeze extra)
Tomato Puree: 28oz (Good tomatoes = good sauce, find the one you like)
Water: 1/3c
Garlic: 1tsp
Salt: 3/4tsp
Black Pepper: Pinch
Sugar: 1tsp
Red Pepper: Pinch
Olive Oil: 2tsp
Oregano: 1tsp

Cheese:
Mozzarella (whole milk, not part-skim – low moisture is ok – full block, not shredded): 8oz total for both pies

Directions:

Dough:
1. Mix all dry ingredients
2. Place water (cool to room temperature) in a large bowl
3. Add half of dry ingredients to water, mix to combine and leave alone for 10 minutes to allow flour to hydrolyze. This lets the water break the flour into its component sugars for the yeast to digest.
4. Mix vigorously for 3 to 5 minutes. Mixing at this stage is more efficient than kneading after all the flour is incorporated.
5. Add the rest of the dry ingredients slowly until all is incorporated into a dough ball.
6. Add olive oil and knead in bowl for 3 to 5 minutes – while mixing was great for the first half of the flour, we still have to work the second half of the flour during this stage.
7. Divide into 2 balls and place dough in plastic bags. Refrigerate for 24-72 hours. (Longer slow rise will yield a more flavorful dough).

Sauce:
8. Mix all sauce ingredients.

Assembly:
9. Remove dough from refrigerator at least 1 hour prior to cooking.
10. Preheat oven with pizza stone on top shelf to 550 degrees (F) for at least 1 hour.
11. Grate mozzarella and place in freezer while preparing dough.
12. Stretch each dough ball to approximately 14 inches on floured pizza peel or cutting board (do not roll, the more bubbles in the dough, the better, be gentle).
13. Top (sparingly) with sauce, too much and you will have a soggy pie. A ladle and a half is plenty. Shake peel/board to ensure dough is still loose from board.
14. Remove cheese from freezer and sprinkle onto pies.
15. Slide onto pizza stone in oven and cook for 5-7 minutes or until desired doneness is achieved.
16. Thank me later.
